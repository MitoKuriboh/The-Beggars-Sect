## Introduction

Culture in the Martial Arts Haven is shaped by one overwhelming truth: the Aptitude System determines everything. From the food you eat to the words you speak, your grade defines your world. Yet beneath the official culture, counter-cultures thrive—especially among those the system rejects.

This document covers daily life, customs, language, food, clothing, entertainment, and social norms across Haven's Cradle's different rings.

# DAILY LIFE BY RING

---

## Spire District Life

*The world of S and A grades*

### Morning Ritual

**5:00 AM - The Cultivation Hour**
Spire residents wake before dawn for morning cultivation. Premium chi-infused air is pumped into meditation chambers. Servants prepare cultivation aids—rare herbs, formation-enhanced mats, chi-conducting incense.

**7:00 AM - Breaking Fast**
Breakfast is an elaborate affair. Multiple courses designed to support chi cultivation:
- Chi-infused rice porridge with ginseng
- Spiritual beast meat (enhances different aspects)
- Tea ceremonies with rare leaves
- Fresh fruits never seen in lower rings

**8:00 AM - Training or Duties**
High-grade individuals train under sect masters or perform administrative duties. No manual labor—that's for the lower rings.

### Afternoon Activities

- Sect meetings and political maneuvering
- Advanced technique training
- Scholarly pursuits (calligraphy, poetry, strategy)
- Social engagements with peers

### Evening

- Formal dinners with elaborate etiquette
- Performances at the Golden Sun theatre
- Private cultivation sessions
- Networking at exclusive establishments

### What They Never See

- How food reaches their tables
- Who cleans their streets
- The poverty their system creates
- Anyone below B-grade (unless serving)

## Garrison Ring Life

*The world of soldiers and discipline*

### Morning Ritual

**4:30 AM - Reveille**
Spartans wake to bells. No gradual rising—feet on floor within thirty seconds or face punishment drills.

**5:00 AM - Physical Training**
Group exercises in formation. Running, strength training, combat drills. Rain, shine, or snow—no exceptions.

**6:30 AM - Mess Hall**
Functional food eaten in fifteen minutes. Protein-heavy, no seasoning. Conversation is tactical, never personal.

### Daily Schedule

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 4:30 | Wake |
| 5:00-6:30 | Physical training |
| 6:30-7:00 | Breakfast |
| 7:00-12:00 | Duty rotation |
| 12:00-12:30 | Lunch |
| 12:30-18:00 | Duty rotation |
| 18:00-19:00 | Dinner |
| 19:00-21:00 | Personal time |
| 21:00 | Lights out |

### What Personal Time Means

- Equipment maintenance
- Individual training
- Letters home (censored)
- The Memorial Chapel (for the questioning ones)

### The Unspoken

Spartans don't discuss:
- Orders that felt wrong
- Colleagues who "transferred" after asking questions
- The growing gap between doctrine and reality
- What they do in the Lower Streets

## Commerce Ring Life

*The world of the "normal"*

### Morning Ritual

**6:00 AM - Work Begins**
Shop owners open stalls. Craftsmen begin production. The Commerce Ring wakes to the sound of commerce.

**Breakfast:** Quick, practical
- Street vendor buns
- Tea from corner shops
- Whatever's affordable

### The Market Day

The Open Market operates sunrise to sunset:
- Haggling is expected (start at 60% of asking)
- Relationships matter more than transactions
- Gossip flows with goods
- Everyone knows everyone's business

### Social Life

**Tea Houses** - Where real business happens
- Different houses for different clienteles
- "The Broken Cup" serves anyone (radical)
- Information is the most valuable commodity

**Evening Entertainment**
- Street performers in Performance Square
- Gambling dens (illegal but tolerated)
- Food stalls open until midnight
- Martial demonstrations (approved styles only)

### The Boundaries

Commerce Ring residents:
- Can see the Spire but never enter
- Employ Labor Ring workers
- Fear becoming Outer Ring
- Pretend the system is fair (mostly)

---

## Labor Ring Life

*The world of the forgotten middle*

### Morning Ritual

**4:00 AM - First Shift**
Factory bells ring. Workers shuffle to stations. Breakfast is whatever you brought—there's no break until midday.

### The Work Day

**12-14 hour shifts** in:
- Foundries (metal work)
- Textile mills
- Construction crews
- Warehouse loading

**Lunch:** 30 minutes
- Canteen food (barely nutritious)
- Sitting on whatever's available
- Too tired for conversation

### Evening

After shift ends:
- Walk home (no transport for D-grades)
- Cook whatever's affordable
- Sleep
- Repeat

### What Sustains Them

- Family bonds
- Dreams for children (maybe they'll test higher)
- The Worker's Rest canteen (Auntie's kindness)
- Secret hope that things could change

### The Red Fist Underground

In hidden basements, the Red Fist Society runs:
- Illegal boxing matches
- Martial training for workers
- A network of mutual support
- The seeds of something larger

---

## Outer Ring Life

*The world of the discarded*

### Morning Ritual

**Whenever you wake** - If you slept
- Check your belongings weren't stolen
- Check you weren't attacked while sleeping
- Find food or don't eat
- Survive another day

### There Is No Schedule

Outer Ring residents:
- Beg when they can
- Scavenge when possible
- Steal when necessary
- Fight when unavoidable

### Food Sources

| Source | Reliability | Danger |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Begging in Commerce Ring | Low | Medium (Spartan harassment) |
| The Lean-To Market | Medium | Low |
| Scavenging Rust Heap | Medium | Medium |
| Gang distribution (if affiliated) | High | High (obligation) |
| Beggars Sect sharing | High | Low |

### Social Structure

Without official structure, alternatives emerge:
- **Gang territories** - Protection through submission
- **Beggars Sect network** - Mutual aid and dignity
- **Family clusters** - Blood bonds
- **Survival partnerships** - Temporary alliances

### The Bright Spot Exception

One street maintains:
- Clean buildings
- Functioning well
- Children's school
- Community defense
- Proof another way exists

## Beggars Sect Life

*The hidden world within the world*

### Morning Ritual (Beggar's Corner)

**Dawn - The Common Fire**
Members gather around the fire that never dies. Tea is shared. News is exchanged. The day's begging assignments are discussed.

**Morning Training**
Those learning techniques practice in the courtyard. Elders observe and correct. The Training Ground sees constant use.

### Daily Rhythm

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Dawn | Common fire gathering |
| Morning | Training or cover activities |
| Midday | Begging routes (cover) |
| Afternoon | Return, share findings |
| Evening | Communal meal |
| Night | Advanced training, story time |

### The Begging Cover

During the day, sect members beg like any other F-grade. This serves multiple purposes:
- Maintains cover (just another beggar)
- Gathers information (beggars are invisible)
- Provides actual income
- Keeps members humble

### Evening Culture

After dark, Beggar's Corner transforms:
- Stories of the old masters
- Technique demonstrations
- Philosophy discussions
- Songs that remember the time before

### The Unwritten Rules

1. Share food before eating
2. Never reveal sect secrets outside
3. Protect the Corner above yourself
4. Train every day, no exceptions
5. Remember: having nothing is having everything

# CUSTOMS AND TRADITIONS

---

## Greetings

### By Ring

**Spire District:**
- Formal bows (depth indicates relative status)
- Sect salutes (fist-palm gesture)
- Titles always used ("Elder," "Master," "Director")
- Physical contact inappropriate

**Garrison Ring:**
- Military salute (right fist to left shoulder)
- Rank-based hierarchy strictly observed
- "Commander," "Captain," "Soldier"
- No salute to civilians

**Commerce Ring:**
- Casual bow or head nod
- Handshakes for business
- First names common among peers
- Warmth indicates relationship depth

**Labor Ring:**
- Head nods (too tired for more)
- Nicknames more common than names
- Physical back-slaps among friends
- Greetings keep working rhythm

**Outer Ring:**
- Eye contact assessment (threat?)
- Gang signs if affiliated
- Beggars nod to each other
- Strangers ignored until proven safe

### The Beggars Greeting

Between sect members:
- Open palms shown (no weapons)
- Slight bow (equals)
- Optional: touch the Beggar's Pine pattern
- Words: "Empty hands, full heart"

## Food Culture

### Spire District Cuisine

**Philosophy:** Food enhances cultivation
**Style:** Complex, multi-course, beautiful

**Signature Dishes:**
- *Dragon's Breath Soup* - Chi-infused bone broth, 48-hour preparation
- *Five Element Rice* - Each grain from different terrain
- *Phoenix Egg Custard* - From spiritual beasts (rumored)
- *Immortal's Tea* - Leaves from chi-rich mountain peaks

**Dining Rules:**
- Wait for highest rank to begin
- Use correct chopstick etiquette
- Comment on the food's cultivation benefits
- Never finish everything (implies host provided too little)

### Commerce Ring Cuisine

**Philosophy:** Food is pleasure and fuel
**Style:** Hearty, flavorful, social

**Popular Foods:**
- *Noodle soups* - Every stall has a secret recipe
- *Dumplings* - Steamed, fried, or souped
- *Roasted meats* - Hung in shop windows
- *Sweet buns* - Breakfast of the working class
- *Tea* - Social lubricant

**Eating Habits:**
- Street food eaten standing
- Restaurants for business
- Tea houses for gossip
- Family meals at home

### Labor Ring Cuisine

**Philosophy:** Food is survival
**Style:** Cheap, filling, fast

**Common Meals:**
- *Congee* - Rice porridge stretched thin
- *Cabbage everything* - Cheapest vegetable
- *Mystery meat buns* - Don't ask
- *Day-old bread* - From Commerce Ring bakeries
- *Water* - Tea is a luxury

**The Canteen:**
Auntie at the Worker's Rest provides:
- Subsidized meals
- Extra portions for families
- No questions about payment delays
- The closest thing to dignity

### Outer Ring Cuisine

**Philosophy:** Food is whatever you can get
**Style:** Survival

**Food Sources:**
- Scavenged Commerce Ring scraps
- Hunted rats and pigeons
- Stolen vegetables
- Shared sect provisions
- Rotgut alcohol (numbs everything)

**The Lean-To Market:**
- Stolen goods resold cheap
- Food of questionable origin
- No health standards
- Sometimes surprisingly good

### Beggars Sect Food

**Philosophy:** "Share first, eat second"
**Style:** Communal, equal portions

**Traditions:**
- Common pot (everyone contributes)
- Elders serve themselves last
- Guests receive best portions
- Hungry Stance practitioners eat less (training)

**Signature Foods:**
- *Beggar's Chicken* - Wrapped in mud, buried in coals
- *Stone Soup* - Whatever's available
- *Fortune Rice* - Mixed grains, unpredictable
- *Empty Bowl Tea* - Hot water with intention

## Clothing

### By Ring

**Spire District:**
- Flowing robes of silk and rare fabrics
- Sect colors proudly displayed
- Jade accessories common
- Impractical designs (no manual labor needed)

**Garrison Ring:**
- Uniform strictly regulated
- Rank insignia prominently displayed
- Practical, durable materials
- Personal expression forbidden

**Commerce Ring:**
- Practical but presentable
- Quality indicates wealth
- Merchant guild emblems
- Fashion follows Spire trends (delayed)

**Labor Ring:**
- Functional, patched, worn
- Work-appropriate (no flowing sleeves near machines)
- Hand-me-downs common
- Children wear adult clothes modified

**Outer Ring:**
- Whatever can be scavenged
- Layered for warmth and concealment
- Gang colors if affiliated
- Patches on patches

### Beggars Sect Clothing

**The Philosophy:**
Appear poor, be prepared.

**Standard Elements:**
- Patched robes (patches hide reinforcement)
- Hidden pockets for weapons/supplies
- Loose fit (allows movement)
- Dull colors (don't attract attention)

**What's Hidden:**
- Sleeves reinforced for blocking
- Hems weighted for weapon use
- Interior pockets for sect items
- Soles with hidden compartments

## Festivals and Observances

### Official Festivals

**Redistribution Day (New Year)**
*Day 1 of Year*
- Celebrates Wei Zhong's "gift" to humanity
- Mandatory attendance at Spire ceremonies
- Testing ceremonies for 5-year-olds
- Fireworks visible from all rings
- Lower rings: reminder of their place

**Array Founding Festival**
*Day 147 (Midyear)*
- Honors the system's creation
- Martial demonstrations (approved sects)
- Grade-based seating at events
- Propaganda performances
- Lower rings: forced cheerfulness

**Harvest Gratitude**
*Day 280*
- Thanks for the system's provision
- Food distribution (grade-proportional)
- Lower rings receive scraps
- Reminds everyone who controls resources

### Underground Observances

**The Remembering**
*Various dates, secret*
- Beggars Sect memorial
- Names of fallen read aloud
- Techniques demonstrated in honor
- The prophecy recited

**Empty Bowl Night**
*Monthly, new moon*
- Fast from sunset to sunrise
- Meditation on emptiness
- Hungry Stance practitioners' special training
- Shared silence, then shared stories

**The Finding**
*Anniversary of Wei Zhong's "death"*
- Elder-only observance
- Discussion of prophecy progress
- Planning for the future
- Ritual at the Archive

### Personal Observances

**Testing Day Anniversary**
Everyone remembers the day they were graded. How they mark it varies:
- Spire: Celebration of status
- Commerce: Family gatherings
- Labor: Quiet acknowledgment
- Outer: Bitter memories
- Beggars: "The day they lied to us"

## Social Norms

### The Unwritten Rules

**What Everyone Knows:**

1. **Grade determines worth** - Officially
2. **Don't question the Array** - Dangerous
3. **Know your place** - Literally (which ring)
4. **Sect loyalty supreme** - For those in sects
5. **The Spartans are always right** - Publicly

**What Everyone Pretends Not to Know:**

1. The system has exceptions
2. F-grades can fight
3. Something's wrong with the Array
4. The Spire hides things
5. Change might be coming

### Interaction Across Grades

**High to Low:**
- Acknowledgment optional
- Commands expected to be obeyed
- Physical contact: their choice
- Eye contact: lower grade should look down

**Low to High:**
- Deference required
- Speak only when addressed
- Physical space maintained
- Eye contact: brief, submissive

**Equal Grade:**
- Normal interaction possible
- Competition acceptable
- Friendships form naturally
- Business partnerships common

### Gender in Haven

Gender exists but doesn't override grade:
- An A-grade woman outranks a B-grade man
- Martial ability is gender-neutral
- Traditional roles exist but aren't enforced
- The Beggars Sect is fully egalitarian

### Age and Respect

Age commands respect within grade:
- Elder martial artists venerated
- "Elder" title earned through achievement
- Young prodigies still defer to aged masters
- Wisdom valued alongside strength

# RELIGION AND PHILOSOPHY

---

## Official Doctrine

### The Array Faith

The closest thing to official religion:
- Wei Zhong as prophet/founder
- The Array as divine instrument
- Grade as spiritual truth
- Hierarchy as natural order
- Questioning as heresy

### What's Taught

- Your grade reflects your soul
- The system protects the weak
- Attempting beyond your grade is dangerous
- The hierarchy maintains peace
- Before the Array: chaos and death

---

## Martial Philosophy

### The Five Principles (Orthodox)

1. **Know Your Limits** - The Array shows truth
2. **Serve the Strong** - Hierarchy protects
3. **Train Within Grade** - Safety first
4. **Honor Your Sect** - Loyalty above all
5. **Maintain Order** - Chaos is the enemy

### The Beggars Counter-Principles

1. **Limits Are Lies** - The Array is incomplete
2. **Serve the Worthy** - Not the strong, the good
3. **Train Beyond Measure** - Growth has no ceiling
4. **Honor Your Path** - Individual truth
5. **Embrace Change** - Stagnation is death

---

## Folk Beliefs

### Common Superstitions

- Bad luck to test on Redistribution Day (your parents did)
- Jade wards off evil chi
- Dreams of water mean change coming
- Black cats in the Outer Ring: death omen
- Finding a coin: someone's thinking of you

### Beggar Beliefs

- The fire that never dies protects the Corner
- Old Dao can appear anywhere he's needed
- The Dog Beating Staff calls to its owner
- Inverse chi flows toward destiny
- The Empty Vessel will be filled

---

## Death and Remembrance

### Official Practices

**Cremation** is standard:
- Ashes kept by family or sect
- Memorial tablets in sect halls
- Names recorded in registers
- Higher grades: elaborate ceremonies
- Lower grades: functional disposal

### Outer Ring Practices

**The Bone Yard:**
- Burial for those with nothing
- Unmarked graves common
- Grandmother Bone tends the dying
- Ash handles cremation
- The Weeping Angel receives prayers

### Beggars Sect Practices

**The Remembering:**
- Names read at the Common Fire
- Technique demonstrated in honor
- Cloth strips tied to the Beggar's Pine
- "They walk with us still"
- No body required (often there isn't one)
