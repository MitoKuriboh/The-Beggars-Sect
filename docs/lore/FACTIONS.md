## Overview

Four major factions shape the world of Haven's Cradle. Each has deep history, internal conflicts, and complex relationships with the others. None are purely good or evil—they are human institutions with human flaws.

---

## THE BEGGARS SECT (丐幫)

*"The empty bowl holds the universe."*

### Identity

The Beggars Sect is the oldest martial organization in the land, predating the Aptitude System by centuries. They are:

- **Outsiders by choice** - Rejecting wealth, status, hierarchy
- **Masters by merit** - Your skill matters, not your birth
- **Family by oath** - Blood means nothing; loyalty means everything
- **Hidden in plain sight** - Everyone sees beggars; no one sees the Sect

### History

#### The Founding (Unknown, ~500 years ago)

Legend says the founder was a prince who gave away his kingdom after achieving enlightenment. He wandered for forty years, learning that:

- Possessions are chains
- Status is illusion
- True strength comes from emptiness
- The lowest can become highest

He codified his insights into two disciplines:
1. **The Eighteen Dragon-Subduing Palms** - Empty-hand combat
2. **The Dog Beating Staff** - The beggar's weapon

The techniques work through "inverse cultivation"—they grow stronger as the user has less.

#### The Golden Age (500-147 years ago)

For centuries, the Sect was respected even by those who despised beggars. Their masters were legendary:

- **Old Beggar Hong** - Said to have defeated an army with his staff
- **Sister Empty** - First female Sect Leader, revolutionized palm techniques
- **Laughing Zhang** - Turned the Dog Beating Staff into an art form
- **Nine-Finger Wang** - Created the Flowing Stance after losing a finger

The Sect maintained the largest intelligence network in the land. Every city had beggars; every beggar had ears. Kings consulted them. Criminals feared them.

#### The Fall (Year 1-20)

When the Aptitude Array arrived, everything changed. The Sect's philosophy—that anyone could become a master—was declared:

- Scientifically impossible
- Socially dangerous
- Evidence of collective delusion

The High Sects declared the Beggars Sect's techniques "incompatible with measured aptitude" and seized their manuals. Members were:

- Tested and humiliated (most showed F-grade)
- Forbidden from teaching
- Hunted if they resisted

The Sect fractured. Some fought and died. Some surrendered and were absorbed into the system. Some—the true believers—went underground.

#### The Hiding (Year 20-100)

The survivors preserved what they could:
- Oral transmission of techniques
- Hidden caches of manuals
- Secret meeting places
- The fundamental philosophy

They learned to appear harmless. A beggar is invisible. A crazy old man talking about martial arts is a joke. They weaponized contempt.

#### The Now (Year 147)

The Sect exists in shadows. Perhaps a few thousand members across the land, a few hundred in Haven's Cradle. They:

- Maintain secret gathering places (like Beggar's Corner)
- Train carefully selected disciples
- Wait for... something
- Pass down the prophecy: "One will come from nowhere, remembering nothing"

### Internal Structure

**Leadership:**
- No formal leader (the last Sect Leader died in Year 45)
- Elders guide through wisdom, not command
- Decisions made by consensus
- Anyone can refuse any request

**Ranks (informal):**
1. **Beggar** - New member, learning philosophy
2. **Brother/Sister** - Accepted member, knows basics
3. **Palm Brother/Sister** - Combat-trained
4. **Elder** - Master of techniques, teacher
5. **Chief Elder** - Unofficial head (currently vacant)

**The Burden System:**
Members carry "burdens"—cloth bags tied to their robes. More burdens = more responsibility. Elders carry nine. New members carry one. Li Wei starts with none.

### Philosophy

**Core Tenets:**

1. **Possess Nothing** - Attachment is weakness
2. **Accept Everything** - What comes, comes
3. **Share Always** - What you have belongs to all
4. **Judge Never** - All people contain all possibilities
5. **Endure Forever** - Suffering passes; the Sect remains

**The Paradoxes:**
- "Full emptiness" - Having nothing means having everything
- "Weak strength" - Being weak lets you become truly strong
- "Lone together" - Individual wandering, collective identity
- "Hidden visible" - Seen by all, known by none

### Internal Conflicts

**The Purists vs. The Pragmatists:**
- Purists: Maintain traditions exactly, wait for prophecy
- Pragmatists: Adapt to survive, actively recruit
- Elder Chen: Pragmatist (sees promise in Li Wei)
- Elder Wu: Purist (doubts easy solutions)

**The Question of Action:**
- Some elders believe it's time to emerge from hiding
- Others believe emergence means destruction
- The debate has lasted decades
- Li Wei's arrival may force a decision

### Secret Knowledge

**What Only Elders Know:**
- The complete Eighteen Dragon-Subduing Palms (only 12 techniques survive publicly)
- The location of the Dog Beating Staff
- The true prophecy (longer than the version others know)
- Why the founder left his kingdom (it wasn't enlightenment)

**What No One Knows:**
- The founder didn't give away his kingdom—it was destroyed
- The Aptitude Array was based on stolen Beggar Sect research
- The Dog Beating Staff chooses its wielder, not the other way around
- Li Wei has been here before

## THE SPARTANS (斯巴達衛)

*"Order through discipline. Peace through control."*

### Identity

The Spartans are the military arm of Haven's Cradle's ruling structure. They are:

- **Enforcers of order** - Maintaining the Aptitude System
- **Believers in hierarchy** - Rank is earned, then sacred
- **Protectors and oppressors** - Depending on who you ask
- **Products of their training** - Made, not born

### History

#### Origin (Pre-Redistribution)

The Spartans began as a volunteer guard force protecting trade caravans. They were:

- Open to anyone willing to train
- Focused on practical defense
- Known for discipline, not cruelty
- Respected but not feared

Their oath: "We stand between the people and the darkness."

#### The Transformation (Year 1-20)

When the Aptitude System emerged, the Spartans faced a choice: adapt or be replaced. They chose adaptation.

**The New Spartans:**
- Recruitment limited to B-grade and above aptitude
- Training standardized around "optimized" techniques
- Mission expanded from protection to enforcement
- Oath rewritten: "We maintain order through strength"

**The Purge:**
Existing members with "insufficient aptitude" were:
- Offered honorable discharge (most)
- Given administrative roles (some)
- Quietly eliminated (a few who knew too much)

#### The Consolidation (Year 20-100)

The Spartans became the High Sects' tool for:
- Enforcing aptitude-based laws
- Suppressing "unauthorized martial activity"
- Hunting remnants of non-compliant sects
- Maintaining the peace of subjugation

They grew efficient, powerful, and increasingly disconnected from the people they once protected.

#### The Modern Era (Year 100-147)

Three generations have passed. No living Spartan remembers the old oath. They believe:

- The Aptitude System is natural law
- Their authority comes from superiority
- Order requires control
- They are protecting society from chaos

**But doubts creep in:**
- Senior officers remember stories from their grandparents
- The contradiction between "protect" and "oppress" creates friction
- Some question why they hunt beggars who threaten no one
- Commander Vex is not the only one who wonders

### Internal Structure

**Hierarchy:**
1. **Recruit** - In training (3 years)
2. **Soldier** - Basic certified
3. **Warrior** - Combat specialist
4. **Sergeant** - Squad leader
5. **Lieutenant** - Platoon commander
6. **Captain** - Company commander
7. **Commander** - District authority
8. **High Commander** - Reports to High Sects

**Units:**
- **The Watch** - Patrol and peacekeeping
- **The Hunt** - Tracking "unauthorized martial artists"
- **The Guard** - Protecting High Sect interests
- **The Academy** - Training new recruits

### Philosophy

**Official Doctrine:**

1. **Discipline is Strength** - Control yourself; control everything
2. **Order is Peace** - Chaos kills; structure saves
3. **Hierarchy is Natural** - Leaders lead; followers follow
4. **Duty is Sacred** - Your role matters; fill it perfectly
5. **Weakness is Threat** - The strong must manage the weak

**The Contradiction:**
Many Spartans genuinely believe they help people. They:
- Prevent crime (by controlling everyone)
- Maintain peace (through fear)
- Protect the weak (from themselves)
- Uphold justice (as defined by the powerful)

### Internal Conflicts

**The Old Guard vs. The True Believers:**
- Old Guard: Remember (secondhand) the original purpose
- True Believers: Accept current doctrine completely
- Commander Vex: Old Guard (which makes him dangerous to the system)
- Most young Spartans: True Believers

**The Question of the Beggars:**
Some Spartans ask:
- Why do we hunt people who have nothing?
- What threat do begging martial artists pose?
- Is an F-grade really incapable of growth?
- Are we protectors or oppressors?

These questions are not asked publicly.

### Commander Vex

**Background:**
- Born to a Spartan family (third generation)
- Tested at A-grade (rare, prestigious)
- Rose through ranks on merit and discipline
- Known for fairness, intelligence, dedication
- Internally conflicted about his role

**His Secret:**
Vex keeps a journal. In it, he records inconsistencies in aptitude testing—cases where low-grade individuals displayed abilities they "shouldn't" have. He doesn't know what to do with this information.

**His Arc:**
Meeting Li Wei will force Vex to choose: defend a system he doubts or acknowledge truths that could destroy his world.

## THE URBAN THUGS

*"Strength takes. Weakness gives."*

### Identity

The gangs of the Lower Streets are not an organization but an ecosystem. They are:

- **Survivors turned predators** - Victims who became victimizers
- **Order from chaos** - Structure where society provides none
- **Family by necessity** - Bonds forged in desperation
- **Products of the system** - What happens when people are discarded

### History

#### Formation (Year 20-50)

As the Redistribution pushed low-aptitude individuals to the edges, social structures collapsed. With no protection, no opportunity, no hope, the strong dominated the weak.

Early gangs were survival collectives:
- Shared food and shelter
- Protected territory from outsiders
- Led by whoever could fight

#### Evolution (Year 50-100)

Gangs became more organized:
- Territories formalized
- Leadership structures emerged
- "Businesses" developed (smuggling, extortion, protection)
- Codes of conduct (honor among thieves)

The Spartans allowed this. Gangs controlled the Lower Streets better than patrols could, and their chaos didn't threaten the upper rings.

#### The Gang Wars (Year 100-110)

Multiple gangs fought for dominance. The violence was apocalyptic:
- Thousands died
- Entire blocks burned
- Civilians caught between
- The Spartans watched

Eventually, two powers remained: the Iron Dogs and the Razors.

#### Razor's Rise (Year 135-147)

A seventeen-year-old called Razor (born name: Li Xing) killed the Iron Dogs' leader in single combat. He:

- Was F-grade (tested as a child)
- Shouldn't have been able to fight
- Clearly could fight
- United both gangs through fear and charisma

No one knows how an F-grade became so strong. Razor doesn't explain.

### Internal Structure

**Hierarchy:**
1. **Street Punk** - New, unproven, expendable
2. **Brawler** - Proven fighter, earns a share
3. **Enforcer** - Trusted, handles serious business
4. **Lieutenant** - Leads crews, answers to Razor
5. **Razor** - Absolute authority

**Territories:**
- Streets are divided into blocks
- Lieutenants control blocks
- Razor controls lieutenants
- Boundaries are marked and respected

**The Code:**
- Don't steal from other gang members
- Don't cooperate with Spartans
- Don't touch children (even gangs have limits)
- What Razor says, goes

### Philosophy

**The Creed:**
"The city threw us away. Now we take from the city. You're either predator or prey. We chose predator."

**The Justification:**
Most gang members don't see themselves as evil. They believe:
- The system made them this way
- They only take from those who can afford it (mostly)
- They protect their own
- Survival isn't a crime

**The Truth:**
They're both victims and victimizers. The cycle perpetuates:
- Thugs prey on beggars
- Beggars' children become thugs
- Thugs prey on the new beggars
- Repeat

### Razor: A Character Study

**Public Image:**
Brutal, efficient, fearless. He takes what he wants and destroys anyone who challenges him. His name is a warning.

**Private Reality:**
Razor taught himself to read using stolen books. He keeps them in a locked room. He writes poetry that no one sees.

He became what he is because:
- At seven, his parents were killed by a gang
- At ten, he joined a gang to survive
- At fourteen, he killed his first man (self-defense)
- At seventeen, he realized he could lead or be led

**His Philosophy:**
"The strong rule. That's how it is. The Spartans, the High Sects—they're just gangs with better PR. At least I'm honest about what I am."

**His Blindspot:**
The Beggars Sect terrifies him because they offer something he can't: dignity without power. If their way works, what does that say about his choices?

### Internal Conflicts

**Razor vs. Reality:**
Razor is smart enough to know the gang isn't sustainable. Eventually:
- The Spartans might decide to purge
- A stronger rival might emerge
- His own lieutenants might turn
- He might just get old

He doesn't have a plan for "after." He's not sure there is one.

**The Question of the Beggars:**
Some gang members notice:
- Beggars don't fight back—but they survive
- The Sect has lasted longer than any gang
- There might be another way

These members sometimes disappear. They joined the Sect.

## THE LONE WOLVES

*"The path is walked alone."*

### Identity

Lone Wolves aren't an organization—they're the absence of one. They are:

- **Individuals beyond faction** - Rejecting all affiliation
- **Seekers of something** - Enlightenment, power, death
- **Mirrors for protagonists** - What you might become
- **The only truly free** - Or the only truly lost

### History

Lone Wolves have always existed. They are:

**Former Sect Members:**
- Expelled for transgression
- Departed over disagreement
- Survived their sect's destruction
- Completed training but found no purpose

**Natural Outliers:**
- High aptitude individuals who rejected their destiny
- Low aptitude individuals who achieved impossible strength
- Those who simply don't fit anywhere

**The Disillusioned:**
- Masters who found no meaning in mastery
- Warriors who tired of war
- Teachers with no students
- Seekers who never found

### The Spectrum of Lone Wolves

**The Honorable:**
- Travel helping those in need
- Challenge worthy opponents for growth
- Seek to perfect their art
- May accept temporary students

**The Neutral:**
- Just want to be left alone
- Don't help or harm unless necessary
- Often hermits or wanderers
- May have profound wisdom or just damage

**The Dangerous:**
- Hunt worthy opponents (who may not want to fight)
- Test themselves through conflict
- May not distinguish between "challenge" and "attack"
- Walking natural disasters

**The Hollow:**
- Achieved mastery, found it empty
- Seek something to fill the void
- May be enlightened or may be broken
- The most unpredictable of all

### The Hollow One

**Origin (Unknown):**
No one knows where the Hollow One came from. They:
- Appeared approximately thirty years ago
- Have never stated a name, birthplace, or background
- Display mastery of multiple styles (contradictory)
- Tested as "unreadable" by the Array

**The Quest:**
The Hollow One has spent three decades seeking "the perfect technique"—something beyond style, beyond form, beyond the limits of measured aptitude.

They have:
- Defeated countless masters
- Studied dozens of styles
- Found each one insufficient
- Grown increasingly... empty

**Current State:**
The Hollow One resides in the mountains beyond Haven's Cradle. They:
- Meditate for days without moving
- Accept challengers who find them
- Defeat all challengers without killing
- Speak in koans that may or may not be wisdom

**What They Seek:**
Not victory. Not technique. Not enlightenment in the traditional sense.

They seek an opponent—or student—who can show them something new. Someone who fights differently. Someone who learned outside the system.

Someone like Li Wei.

**The Knowledge:**
The Hollow One knows:
- Where the Dog Beating Staff is hidden
- The truth about the Aptitude Array
- Something about Li Wei's past
- More than they say

### Lone Wolves as Narrative Device

Each Lone Wolf Li Wei encounters represents a possible future:

- **The Wandering Fighter** - What if you sought only to grow?
- **The Silent Master** - What if you found peace in solitude?
- **The Hollow One** - What if you achieved everything and found nothing?

They are mirrors: "Is this who you want to become?"

Conflict: Thugs prey on beggars

Respect: Both reject the system in their own ways
Caution: Lone Wolves are unpredictable
Possibility: Some Lone Wolves were once Sect members

**Thugs → Lone Wolves:**
Fear: Lone Wolves are above gang politics
Avoidance: Don't antagonize someone who can destroy you
Aspiration: Some thugs dream of becoming strong enough to leave

## Faction Evolution Through the Story

### Chapter 1: Establishment

- **Beggars Sect:** Introduced as sanctuary and family
- **Thugs:** Primary antagonists (Razor)
- **Spartans:** Background threat, distant
- **Lone Wolves:** Not yet encountered

### Chapter 2: Expansion

- **Beggars Sect:** Philosophy deepened, challenges emerge
- **Thugs:** Receding threat (Razor defeated but not forgotten)
- **Spartans:** Primary antagonists (Commander Vex)
- **Lone Wolves:** First encounters (Wandering Fighter)

### Chapter 3: Convergence

- **Beggars Sect:** Preparing for emergence or concealment
- **Thugs:** Absent (their chapter is past)
- **Spartans:** Vex's questioning reaches crisis
- **Lone Wolves:** The Hollow One as final challenge

### Future (Post-MVP):

- **Beggars Sect:** Must choose: remain hidden or emerge
- **Thugs:** Potential allies against greater threats
- **Spartans:** Possible schism between reformers and loyalists
- **Lone Wolves:** Li Wei may become one—or unite them
